Aurora Tech: Data Governance Policy

Executive Presentation

  • Objective: Secure and govern supply-chain data for margin prediction.
  • Module: Bloc 1 - Data Governance
  • Speaker: Data Architect Candidate
  • Duration: 15 minutes Defense
Slide 1 / 10

1. Context & Objectives

Why govern our data?

  • Financial margin predictions require absolute data accuracy.
  • Regulatory Compliance (GDPR/CCPA) for vendor contracts and logistics.
  • Internal security: Mitigate risks of unauthorized access to cost margins.
Slide 2 / 10

2. Key Stakeholders

Who is involved?

  • Chief Data Officer (CDO): Strategy & Sponsorship
  • Data Stewards: Logistics & Finance Dept. Managers
  • Data Owners: IT Infrastructure Team
  • Data Consumers: MLOps teams, Supply Chain Analysts
Slide 3 / 10

3. GDPR & Compliance

Regulatory alignment

  • Data Minimization: PII removed from cost-prediction datasets.
  • Consent & Contracts: DPA added to vendor agreements.
  • Right to Erasure: Automated anonymization scripts.
Slide 4 / 10

4. Data Quality Standards

Ensuring accurate models

  • Completeness: Null values in FX rates trigger fallback APIs.
  • Accuracy: Real-time cross-checking with secondary sources.
  • Timeliness: Midnight batch ingestion SLA.

6. Risk Assessment Matrix

Identifying vulnerabilities

  • Risk: Third-party API failure (e.g., FX API goes down). Mitigation: Caching & Moving averages.
  • Risk: Data Breach of supplier costs. Mitigation: Encryption at Rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS).
Slide 7 / 10

7. Training & Awareness

Cultural adoption

  • Mandatory annual security awareness modules for all staff.
  • Specific Data Steward workshops to define and manage glossaries.
  • Phishing simulation tests for high-risk roles (Finance).
Slide 8 / 10

8. Governance Audit Plan

Continuous improvement

  • Quarterly internal audits on RBAC permissions and access logs.
  • Annual external penetration tests.
  • Automated data quality scorecards reviewed monthly.
